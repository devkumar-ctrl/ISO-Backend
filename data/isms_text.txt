Information Technology is the ubiquitous common thread that runs across and connects all the company’s business activities. As a company, DamnArt is wholly dependent on computer systems to carry on all its client servicing activities and could be exposed to the risks pertaining to IT if appropriate protection and prevention mechanisms are not developed, implemented and complied with. Information Security Criteria Availability : All Information Systems , including hardware, communication networks, software programs and the data they hold will be available to all those users who need the systems at all times they are needed, to carry out business activities. Confidentiality : No data or information will be disclosed to any person within or outside the company, other than the persons who are authorized to use that data. Data Integrity : No data/information/programs will be allowed to be modified by anyone without proper authority and authorizations. This will ensure the accuracy and completeness of information and processing methods. No data will be modified , added , edited , or deleted except by users or programs that are authorized to do so and in a manner as approved or designed. Risk A combination of the probability of an event and its consequence Source An item or activity having the potential for a consequence Risk Criteria Terms of reference by which the significance of risk is assessed- e.g. cost and benefits, legal requirements, environmental aspects, concerns of stakeholders. Risk management Coordinated the activity of risk assessment, treatment, acceptance, and communication. Risk Analysis Systematic use of information (historical data, theoretical analysis, informed opinions, concerns of stakeholders) to identify sources and estimate risks. Information security incident A single of series of unwanted or unexpected events that have significant probability of compromising business operations and threatening information security Integrity The property of safeguarding the accuracy and completeness of asset Confidentiality The property that information is not made available of disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities or processes Availability The property of being accessible and usable upon demand by authorized users ISCG ( Information Security Core Group) The core group is responsible for implementing policies and controls and reviewing information security incidents and policies HR System Admin Technical Head CEO 3.0. ACCEPTABLE USE OF ASSETS POLICY PURPOSE : The explicit publication of this policy in the ISMS policy document is to Emphasize to all employees the importance of Acceptable use of all assets. Protect the privacy, confidentiality , and security of DamnArt’s information. To reduce incidents of inappropriate use. SCOPE: All Employees POLICY Individuals must use DamnArt’s provided or authorized information technology resources as the business tools required to do their work and provide efficient service delivery. Do’s Maintain vigilant awareness against phishing attempts by verifying the sender's identity before clicking links or downloading attachments; Ensure Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is enabled on all eligible accounts to prevent unauthorized access from compromised credentials . Ensure Antivirus is installed in the system. Use a company-approved password manager to generate and store complex, unique passwords for every service . Adhere to endpoint security compliance by keeping all operating systems and applications updated with the latest security patches . Report any suspicious activities or potential endpoint compromises to the security team immediately for incident response . Create complex passwords (mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols) and at least 12 characters long . change passwords at regular intervals or based on the number of accesses (passwords for privileged accounts should be changed more frequently than normal passwords), and avoid re-using or cycling old passwords; terminate active sessions when finished, unless they can be secured by an appropriate locking mechanism, e.g. , a password-protected screen saver; log off mainframe computers, servers, and office PCs when the session is finished (i.e. , not just switch off the PC screen or terminal); secure PCs or terminals from unauthorized use by a key lock or an equivalent control, e.g. , password access, when not in use Don’ts Attempt to circumvent or subvert system or network security measures; Propagate viruses knowingly or maliciously; Detrimentally affect the productivity, integrity , or security of DamnArt ’s systems; Access a personal external email account (e.g., Gmail ) from a DamnArt workstation for reasons unrelated to DamnArt’s business; Access social networking websites (e.g. Facebook, Instagram, Telegram, spotify, Snapchat ) for reasons unrelated to DamnArt’s business; Obtain or distribute files from unauthorized or questionable sources (e.g., racist material, pornography, file-swapping sites); Access Internet sites that might bring the public service into disrepute or harm DamnArt’s reputation, such as those that carry offensive material; Access radio stations or video clips (typically referred to as &quot;streaming&quot; audio or video) over the Internet, unless the access is work-related and approved by a DamnArt’s Manager; Download non-work-related files, such as Freeware, Shareware, movie , games or music files; Divulge, share or compromise their own or another's DamnArt authentication credentials; Transmit or expose sensitive or personal information to the internet by DamnArt ’s network ; Use information and technology resources for commercial solicitation or for conducting or pursuing business interests unrelated to the business of DamnArt ; Distribute hoaxes, chain letters, or advertisements; Send r ude, obscene or harassing messages; Attempt to obscure the origin of any message or download material under an assumed internet address; Knowingly enable inappropriate levels of information access by others; Disclose any information you do not have a right to disclose. avoid keeping a record (e.g. paper, software file or hand-held device) of passwords, unless this can be stored securely and the method of storing has been approved; not include passwords in any automated log-on process, e.g. stored in a macro or function key; not share individual user passwords; not use the same password for business and non-business purposes. PHYSICAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL SECURITY SECURE AREAS PHYSICAL SECURITY PERIMETER: PHYSICAL SECURITY PERIMETER: The office is located on the ground floor at SCO No. 09, Aero View Plaza, Airport Road, Zirakpur. The premises are situated at an approximate elevation of 305 meters above mean sea level. The building is a multi-story commercial structure with controlled access points, including a primary entrance monitored for security purposes. PHYSICAL ENTRY CONTROLS: The premises have only one gat e. SECURING OFFICES ROOMS AND FACILITIES: The organization operates from a single office unit with a single controlled entry point. There are no segregated restricted areas or biometric systems implemented. Access to the premises is regulated through the main entrance, and only authorized personnel are allowed entry. Visitors are permitted under supervision. Basic physical security measures are in place to prevent unauthorized access. PROTECTION AGAINST EXTERNAL AND ENVIRONMENTAL THREATS: The office is situated on the ground floor of a commercial building with adequate structural stability. At present, dedicated fire suppression systems (such as fire extinguishers) and CCTV surveillance are not deployed within the office premises. The organization relies on the building’s common safety provisions where applicable. Management acknowledges this as an area for improvement and plans to enhance physical security controls in the future. EQUIPMENT SECURITY All Computers are sited safely at least 2 inches above the ground. The equipment are sited to prevent physical threats like thefts, vibration, water and vandalism. “No Eating/smoking” near any Information Processing facility is allowed. SUPPORTING UTILITIES : The organization has implemented power backup mechanisms to ensure continuity of operations during power interruptions. An inverter system is in place to provide immediate backup for critical systems, including servers and development computers. Additionally, a generator is available to support extended power outages. These measures ensure minimal disruption to business operations. CABLING SECURITY Power and communication cabling are separate. Network cables are identified. Limited alterations to power cabling can be done because the Office is on rent. EQUIPMENT MAINTENANCE All equipments are normally maintained in house . Laptops are sent to service center after confiscation of data for servicing. Delivery challans for the same are maintained. SECURITY OF EQUIPMENT OFF PREMISES: The organization follows a controlled on-premise working model, where employees are provided with desktop systems for use strictly within the office premises. No portable computing devices are issued, thereby minimizing risks related to device loss or unauthorized external access. Organizational data is maintained within the local environment, ensuring better control and security. Access to systems is restricted through password-based authentication, and employees are required to adhere to defined information security practices.
